Best Metal Build Gundam Figures Ranked (2026)

Five premium Gundam figures ranging from $250 to $690. We bought them all. Here's which ones are actually worth the money.

Bandai's premium Gundam lines have gotten expensive. Metal Build figures routinely cross the $300 mark, Perfect Grade kits approach $700, and the Ka Signature line sits somewhere in between. The question isn't whether these figures look good — they all do. The question is which ones justify the price tag.

We got hands on five of the most talked-about premium Gundam releases available right now and ranked them by one criteria: value for what you actually get. Not hype, not rarity, not brand loyalty. Just the figure in your hands versus the number on the receipt.

Rankings
#1 Ex-S Gundam Metal Robot Spirits Ka Signature — Best Overall Value #2 Gundam Barbatos 4th Form Metal Build — Best for IBO Fans #3 Freedom Gundam Concept 2 Metal Build — Best Reissue Pickup #4 Nu Gundam Perfect Grade Unleashed — Best for Builders #5 Burning Gundam Second Metal Build — For Devotees Only
#1

Ex-S Gundam Metal Robot Spirits Ka Signature Action Figure

~$250 Ka Signature Line Hajime Katoki Design Gundam Sentinel

The cheapest figure in this lineup and it punches way above its price. Katoki's splitter camouflage paint is hand-applied detail that photographs like concept art. The alternate red-eyed head for A.L.I.C.E. system activation completely changes the character of the display — it's not a gimmick, it's a second figure. Two beam sabers, beam smart gun, and INCOM cable sets round out an accessory count that rivals figures at twice the price.

For Sentinel fans, it's a no-brainer. For everyone else, this is the best entry point into the Metal Robot Spirits line. At $250, you're getting Ka Signature quality without Metal Build pricing.

Verdict: Best value in the premium Gundam space right now. If you buy one figure from this list, make it this one.

#2

Gundam Barbatos 4th Form with Boomerang Mace Metal Build Action Figure

~$330 Metal Build Die-Cast Frame Iron-Blooded Orphans

The Metal Build Barbatos has a problem. It's heavy. Like, noticeably heavy. The die-cast frame gives it a density that no other Barbatos figure comes close to matching. Put it on a shelf next to the Robot Spirits version and it looks like a completely different character.

The 4th Form is the sweet spot in the Barbatos lineup — aggressive enough to be interesting, not so loaded with armor that it loses the silhouette. The crimson cape is cloth, not plastic, which matters more than you'd think for dynamic poses. Joints are tight out of the box. The Boomerang Mace actually stays in the hand.

Verdict: At $330, you're paying Metal Build tax. But if IBO is your series, this is the definitive Barbatos. Nothing else is close.
Buy at EE
#3

Freedom Gundam Concept 2 Metal Build Action Figure — Reissue

~$350 Metal Build Reissue Gundam Seed

This is a reissue, and that matters. The original Concept 2 Freedom sold out fast and aftermarket prices got ugly. Bandai bringing it back at $350 tells you exactly how well it moved.

The wings are the entire point. Spread out, the Freedom Gundam's wingspan dominates any shelf it's on. Seven inches of figure that takes up the space of something twice its size. Beam rifle and dual sabers are standard Metal Build quality — tight fit, good weight. The wing articulation lets you get dramatic poses without the whole thing collapsing ten minutes later.

Verdict: If you missed it the first time, grab it now. Reissues don't get third runs.
Buy at EE
#4

Nu Gundam Perfect Grade Unleashed 1:60 Scale Model Kit

~$690 Perfect Grade Unleashed 1:60 Scale 40+ Hour Build

Six hundred and ninety dollars for a model kit. Let that sit for a second.

The PG Unleashed Nu Gundam is Bandai Hobby's flagship, built for Gunpla's 45th anniversary. At 1:60 scale, it's enormous — Amuro's RX-93 standing nearly a foot tall when assembled. The multi-colored metallic inner frame is visible through armor gaps, giving it a depth no straight build kit matches. The face parts have a light-emitting option that actually works without looking cheap.

Here's the thing though. This is a builder's kit, not a collector's shelf piece. You need 40-plus hours and real skill to make this look the way the box art promises. If you've never built a Perfect Grade before, start somewhere else.

Verdict: If you're a seasoned Gunpla builder who wants the crown jewel, this is it. If you're not, this kit will punish you.
Buy at EE
#5

Burning Gundam Second Metal Build Action Figure

~$472 Metal Build 30th Anniversary G Gundam

Four hundred and seventy-two dollars. The most expensive action figure on this list, and it knows it.

The Burning Gundam Second is a 30th anniversary piece supervised by director Yasuhiro Imagawa himself. The metal body isn't cosmetic — it's structural. Pick it up and the weight distribution tells you this was engineered, not just designed. It transforms into the Burning Gundam Second form, and the transformation actually works without feeling like you're about to snap a joint. Five pairs of hands, solar ring effect parts, wing set, and a core lander.

But let's be honest. Nearly five hundred dollars for a seven-inch figure is a hard sell for anyone who isn't a G Gundam devotee. If you are? This is the only version that exists at this level. If you're not? The Ex-S at half the price is the smarter buy.

Verdict: For G Gundam fans only. Everyone else should put this money toward the #1 and #2 picks combined.
